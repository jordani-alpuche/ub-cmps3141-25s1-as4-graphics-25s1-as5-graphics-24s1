Design Principles Applied

1. Repetition

To unify the corporate identity, I used the principle of Repetition extensively. I extracted the curved "wifi signal" shape from the main logo and repeated it as a large, decorative brown element in the bottom corners of both the Business Card and the Envelope. Additionally, I repeated the specific icon set (green outlines) across all three designs to ensure the contact information always feels familiar and consistent.

2. Contrast

I applied Contrast specifically in the typography. For the employee name John Doe, I used a bold, heavy serif font, but for the job title Manager, I switched to a delicate script font. This visual contrast creates an immediate hierarchy, separating the person's identity from their role. I also utilized high color contrast by placing the dark brown text and graphics against the light beige background to ensure readability.

3. Alignment

I used Alignment to keep the layouts clean and professional. On the envelope and business card, the logo and text blocks are strongly left-aligned, creating a sharp invisible line that guides the eye. On the letterhead, I used a horizontal alignment for the footer, balancing the contact information across the width of the page within the green bar.
