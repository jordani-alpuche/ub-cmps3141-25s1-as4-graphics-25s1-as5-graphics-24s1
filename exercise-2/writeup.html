Exercise 2: Color Improvements

I removed the bright blue colors and replaced them with a more warmer set palette following the 60-30-10 rule to improve aesthetics and usability:

  • 60% Dominant (Background): I replaced the stark white background with a warm "Isabelline" (#f4f1ea). This reduces eye strain caused by blue-light glare and gives the site a more organic, welcoming feel.
  • 30% Secondary (Structure): I chose a deep "Charcoal Green" (#264653) for the header, footer, and headings. Removed the previous blue tones to create a strong, stable structure that contrasts well with the dominant color while maintaining a warm aesthetic.
  • 10% Accent (Highlights): I applied a vibrant Terra Cotta (#e76f51) to buttons and focus states. This high-contrast warm color immediately draws the user's eye to key interactions.
